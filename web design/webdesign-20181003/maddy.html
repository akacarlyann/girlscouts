The Periodic Table of Elements

Types of Elements

There are ten types of elements.

  1. Alkali Metals: These metals behave similarly and portray similar characteristics.
  2. Alkaline Earth Metals: These elements have a higher melting point and oxides that have basic alkaline solutions.
  3. Lanthanides: Often called the "f-elements" due to the valence electrons in the f-shell.
  4. Antinides: Also called the "f-elements" because of the valence in their f-shell, but Antinides are all radioactive elements.
  5. Transition Metals: Metals that have partially filled the d-subshell.
  6. Post-Transition Metals: These share similar characteristics to Transition Metals, but they are softer and dont conduct as well.
  7. Metalloids: Sometimes, they behave as semiconductors rather than conductors. They are nicknamed "semi-metals" or "poor metals."
  8. Nonmetals:
  9. Halogens: These are subsets of Nonmetals. Halogens are normally very chemically reactive, and can be found in the environment as compounds instead of pure elements.
  10. Noble Gases: The elements that fall into this category are very stable, and appear without color or smell.
